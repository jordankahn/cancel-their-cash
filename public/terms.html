§1 What this site is

Cancel Their Vote is political satire and a voter-turnout project. It exists to convert petty energy into registered, planned, in-person or by-mail voting. "Canceling" a vote is a joke about arithmetic: every ballot counts exactly once, and yours counts exactly as much as anyone's — famous, rich, loud, or otherwise. The only real-world effect this site aspires to have is you showing up to vote.

§2 No affiliation, no endorsement

No person named on this site sponsors, endorses, approves of, or has anything whatsoever to do with it. Names of public figures are used solely for the purpose of commentary, parody, and political speech about public figures. No photograph, likeness, signature, or trademark of any named person is used.

§3 We don't know how anyone votes

Ballots in the United States are secret. Any implication about how a named person might vote is speculation you brought with you — the site makes no claim about any person's past or future vote, party, or beliefs. "Home states" reflect public reporting about where a person is believed to primarily reside, may be wrong or out of date, and say nothing about where (or whether) anyone is actually registered.

§4 No inducements

Federal law (52 U.S.C. § 10307(c)) prohibits offering or accepting payment for registering to vote or for voting. Good news: this site offers you nothing. No prizes, no sweepstakes, no giveaways, no merch, no coupons. A downloadable image of a fake certificate has a fair market value of $0.00 and we intend to keep it that way. Your pledge is symbolic; your vote is yours.

§5 Not election infrastructure

This site does not register voters, collect registration forms, or handle ballots. All registration links go to vote.gov or official state election offices. For authoritative election information, consult your state or local election authority, or the U.S. Election Assistance Commission. Nothing here literally cancels, alters, blocks, or affects any vote. That is not a thing. Please do not attempt to make it a thing.

§6 Vote pairing precedent, for the curious

Even outright vote-swapping agreements between voters were held to be protected First Amendment activity in Porter v. Bowen, 496 F.3d 1009 (9th Cir. 2007), so long as nothing of value changes hands. This site involves no agreement with anyone — a pledge here is a unilateral statement of your own voting intention, which is core political speech.

§7 Privacy (there's barely anything to have a policy about)

  • We store: an anonymous counter per named public figure, and — only if you type one — a first name (max 20 characters) attached to each pledge, shown in the public activity feed.
  • We do not collect email addresses, last names, precise locations, or contact information. Your state selection is stored in your own browser (localStorage), not on our server.
  • No accounts. No tracking pixels. No analytics cookies. No ad tech. No data sales.
  • Don't type anything into the first-name field you wouldn't put on a bumper sticker.

§8 Conduct

Pledges are jokes with a civic payload. Do not use this site to harass, threaten, or dox anyone. Cancellation is a metaphor. The people listed here are entitled to vote exactly as they wish, and so are you — that symmetry is the entire point.

§9 No warranty

Provided as-is, with all faults, at the quality level you'd expect from a website whose core feature is a rubber stamp. We may remove or change listed names at any time, including on request of the named person, because we are satirists, not jerks.
