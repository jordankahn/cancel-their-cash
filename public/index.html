Instructions to Cancelers

  1. Every ballot in America counts exactly once — famous, rich, loud, or yours. To “cancel” a vote is to show up with the opposite one. That is the entire mechanism. It is also the law of arithmetic.
  2. To cancel, completely fill in the oval beside the name. Use ink. Spite is an acceptable ink.
  3. A famous vote can only be canceled once. Additional cancellations are accepted and filed under Spite, Surplus.
  4. Your cancellation remains PENDING until you cast an actual ballot. A cancellation without a ballot is a birthday wish.

      Part 3 — Notes on methodology

      ¹ On “canceling”

      Votes don't cancel; they count, one each. But if you were going to sit this one out and someone on this form wasn't, they were about to out-vote you 1–0. Voting makes it 1–1. That's the whole bit.

      ² On the surplus

      When 48,000 people cancel one billionaire's single vote, 47,999 cancellations are surplus. The Bureau redirects surplus spite toward the only thing it's good for: getting you to the polls.

      ³ On states

      Midterms are state business — Senate seats, governors, the House. Your ballot only shares races with people registered in your state. That's why the form asks. It's the only personal question on it.

      ⁴ On follow-through

      Registration takes two minutes at vote.gov. Make a plan: when, where, how you're getting there. Voters with a plan actually go. That's the part of this that isn't a joke.

      The fine print. This is satire with a turnout problem to solve. No person named on this form endorses, sponsors, or has anything to do with it. Ballots are secret; any guess about how anyone votes is speculation you brought with you. “Home states” are based on public reporting and may be wrong. No prizes, no rewards, no merch — just arithmetic. We store a counter and, if you offer one, a first name. Nothing else.
